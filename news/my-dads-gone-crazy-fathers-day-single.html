Father’s Day The Shady Way

June 28, 2026

Two of Eminem’s beloved “dad” tracks have been paired for his latest offering: a 7-inch collector’s vinyl in celebration of Father’s Day 2026.

The A-side features the playful father-daughter collaboration “My Dad’s Gone Crazy,” originally released in 2002 on The Eminem Show. The B-side features the heartfelt lullaby-inspired “Mockingbird,” originally released in 2004 on Encore and later issued as the album’s fourth major single in 2005.

The single features artwork for both songs. Despite receiving radio play in 2002, “My Dad’s Gone Crazy” has never had an official cover — until now, with a fitting hockey mask ink-blot design. The new “Mockingbird” artwork, a simplistic childlike drawing, takes inspiration from The Slim Shady LP (and The Death of Slim Shady), using a child-written title in a matching color scheme. It also references the song’s music video through the inclusion of a projector. The vinyl itself — clear with an ink-blot marble effect — is designed to match the single’s A-side.

The collector’s single follows last year’s “Stan” / “Bad Guy” release, which paired the related tracks on a unique rectangular vinyl that also doubled as a replica of Eminem’s original handwritten “Stan” lyric page. It was housed inside a sleeve designed to resemble the envelope addressed to Eminem Fan Mail that Stan sends in the song’s music video.

In what is hopefully a continuing series, future releases could include pairings such as “’97 Bonnie & Clyde / Kim,” “Cleanin’ Out My Closet / Headlights,” or, perhaps the most obvious, “Just Don’t Give a Fuck / Still Don’t Give a Fuck.”
