Words from Eminem
What happened with "Kill You" was I came off the European tour in October of '99 and I called Dre and I told him that I needed some new tracks. […] Meanwhile, there was a track playing in the background. […] He puts the phone to the speaker and it was the "Kill You" beat. I told him, "Send me that shit." […] I recorded it weeks later when I started recording the Marshall Mathers album. The first thing I came up, with was the hook. […] I wanted to start the album with that song because everybody in the press was like "What's he gonna rap about? He's not miserable anymore. He can't rap about being broke no more. […] That’s why I started [the song] with that line. […] The whole hook is basically bashing woman. […] Then at the end of the song I say, “I’m just kidding ladies. You know I love you.” It’s kind of like you can say whatever you want as long as you say you’re joking at the end. […] The whole idea of this song was to say some of the most fucked-up shit. Just to let people know that I’m back. That I didn’t lose it. That I wasn’t compromising nothing and I didn’t change. If anything I got worse. — Eminem, Angry Blonde, 2000
