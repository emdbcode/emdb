Words from Eminem
Growing up, I fought a lot – whether it was a white neighborhood or a black neighborhood, whether it was because of my skin color or because I was small or skinny. But as a kid I hated crossing 8 Mile and going into Warren, into hillbilly territory, and getting called a wigger. The fact that I loved hip-hop — just the fact that I rapped in school – some people thought it was cool and some people thought it was bullshit. But just coming home, crossing 8 Mile and getting jumped – I was tested a lot. — Eminem, Vanity Fair, 2004
