Trivia
The title references the oral analgesic brand Chloraseptic, used to soothe throat and mouth pain. The track itself takes aim at the contemporary generation of rappers, criticizing their tendency to mumble while rapping or singing.
“Chloraseptic” was later remixed with new verses and released as a non-album single, featuring 2 Chainz and Phresher.
2 Chainz was originally expected to feature on the album version after telling XXL he had recorded for the project, shortly after turning down Eminem’s offer to appear on the remix. However, when the tracklist was released, he was unhappy to see he had been cut, sharing it on Instagram with angry and punching emojis. The material he believed was for the album was instead used for the remix, against his wishes, which was released a month later.

Words from Phresher
Paul Rosenberg had made the call. He emailed and called my manager like, “Hey, Eminem wants Phresher on the project. Eminem heard “Wait A Minute.” That’s what made him say, “Yeah, I want this kid on the album, man.”
It was easy. He was pretty much like, “Yo, listen, this is the hook. I want you to lay this down right here.” And then I started to add my own twist and that’s when it got real in there. I started harmonizing under the vocals and he was like, “Oh, nah, no. Hold on. Oh man!” He just got super excited. The whole Shady team was just excited.
Eminem is my favorite rapper. I know he didn’t believe me when I told him but I was just so astonished. He was giving me so much props. I was like, “Nah, I don’t want to talk about me. I’m trying to tell you how I feel about you.” He was just telling me how he felt about me. I was just honored to hear how much he respected what I was doing. I was bugging. 
This was my moment to shine. I did what he wanted me to do but I just put the PH on top of the extra sauce. And when I put the extra sauce, that’s when he was really going crazy. It was amazing.
The record is about just spitting, man. Just going off, you get what I’m saying? Just cutthroat, at your throat music. Like, I’m on you, you know what I’m saying? Real right-at-you music. It’s just awesome, man. Eminem went crazy on it. — Phresher, Complex, 2017
