Words from MAJ
I got a call from my manager, and he was like, "Yo, you would not believe who just did this," or, like, he didn’t even really want to tell me who it was at first, because, you know, Eminem’s a really secretive person when it comes to drops and anything releasing. So [my manager] told me, "I got this reference track from this person." Then he sent me the reference track, and it was saying, "I told my dawgs we gon' get right in a year," and I was like, "Okay, I’d actually fuck with this, I actually wanna try this out."
So the first thing I did was sit on it for a few minutes, and he didn’t even tell me the name of the artist or anything. Then, like, two hours later, I called my manager back and was like, "Yo, you have to tell me who this is." He made me guess so many times, and I was like, "Oh my god, just go ahead and tell me." Then he just straight up told me, "Eminem," and I was like, "Whoa! What!"
Paul Rosenberg, on a phone call, told me that [Eminem] gave him a list of [new] artists, and he had heard one of my songs and was like, "That's the guy," so he picked me, which is insane. Then he gave me a description of how he wanted it because I got the style of it, but I had to make it more jumpy for him. And then he literally was telling me, "I’m so talented, I’m amazingly gifted," and I’m like, "Bro, you’re fuckin’ Eminem, you cannot be telling me all this, bro." I’m just so blessed to have this opportunity and blessed to be a part of the discography. Just being a huge fan, growing up with his music, listening to him when times are low, watching my dad and my mom listening to Eminem around me when I was young, it’s insane that be a part of the shit. — MAJ, BlackySpeakz, 2020
