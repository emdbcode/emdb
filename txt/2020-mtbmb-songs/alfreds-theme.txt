Trivia
Eminem samples the original <em>Alfred Hitchcock Presents</em> television theme by Charles Gounod, rather than the album version by Jeff Alexander from <em>Alfred Hitchcock Presents: Music To Be Murdered By</em>.
Words from Eminem and Paul Rosenberg
It was Paul’s idea to flip that beat, so Paul was like, “Yo, what if you took the Alfred theme and made a beat out of it?” I did. Originally, it was actually the first song recorded for the B-side. We originally talked about maybe just throwing it out there, but then once I started, you know, honing in on the direction I wanted to go and shit, it started kind of becoming its own album, you know. It also tied in with the first one so well. — Eminem, Shad 45, 2020
That theme song for Alfred Hitchcock Presents was something that I thought could make for an amazing beat. I actually said [to Em], “I don’t know if you can flip it into a beat,” and [he] was like, “Oh yeah, I can.” — Paul Rosenberg, Paul Pod: Curtain Call 2, 2022
