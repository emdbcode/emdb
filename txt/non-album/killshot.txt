Trivia
“Killshot” is Eminem’s response to Machine Gun Kelly’s diss track “Rap Devil,” which was released three days after “Not Alike,” in which Eminem took aim at MGK following a 2012 tweet calling his daughter Hailie “hot” when she was just 16; his 2015 blackballing rant on Hot 97, later fleshed out on in his 2017 “LA Leakers Freestyle”; and the shots he took on Tech N9ne’s 2018 track “No Reason.”
“Killshot” is widely regarded as Eminem’s victorious reply.
The song was released as a non-album single on September 14, 2018, peaking at No. 3 on the Billboard Hot 100, No. 13 on the UK Singles Chart, and topping the Canadian Hot 100.
The hip-hop community eagerly anticipated the song’s release, which went on to break the record for the biggest YouTube debut for a hip-hop track, amassing 38.1 million views within 24 hours.
Machine Gun Kelly did not respond with a song, but addressed it through public statements, including rating it 6 out of 10 in a meme and posting a photo of himself on stage wearing a “Killshot” T-shirt, holding up a middle finger with the caption “He missed.”
In the song, Eminem makes reference to Diddy being responsible for the murder of Tupac Shakur, a claim he repeats on his 2024 track “Fuel” and its remix, which contains entirely different lyrics.
