Trivia
Despite being credited to Eminem, Masta Ace, and J-Black, the song did not involve Eminem directly. His vocals originate from Domingo’s 1999 track “Hustlers & Hardcore,” with new vocals recorded by Masta Ace and J-Black for DJ Rob and Domingo’s <em>Game Over</em> compilation.
The song was released as a single in Europe in 2002, finding success in Belgium, peaking at No. 4 and No. 10 in its two regions.
The track was later included on Masta Ace’s compilation album <em>Hits U Missed</em>, crediting Eminem and J-Black as featured artists.
Words From Masta Ace and Eminem
There’s a fake collabo out there, this joint called “Hellbound” - I say fake because we we weren’t in a studio together, it was like two verses thrown together or whatever. It came out on Yosumi, which is my partners and my label that I’m on now. [Em’s vocals are from] a verse that [he] had did for them for some project. — Masta Ace, Shade 45, 2008
I never heard it. Somebody needs to get it to me. — Eminem, Shade 45, 2008
