Words from Denaun Porter
After the early D12 stuff, we kinda disbanded that version of [the group], and I think at that time him and Proof were mad at each other — but they’re best friends. Em is frustrated. We had to move from Fairport because he's a dad. I go back home, and I remember he came to my house — my mom's house — and he said, “Yo, I need some beats. I'm working on this [EP]. This is my last shot. And I'm like, “Man, if I don't get money for beats right now my parents are gonna kick me out again, so I don't give a damn if it's $20.” He’s like, “I ain't got no money but I’ve got a radio.” I said, “Cool, ’cause I ain't got no radio to make beats on.”
So he gave me the radio that he had — and we’re both at our wits’ end, ’cause I gotta go get a job now, ’cause [my parents] didn't even want me doing [music]. They gave me a shot at it — like I moved, we put [Infinite] out, it didn't work — so […] I go make beats on this radio that he gave me, and the first beat that I made was “Low Down, Dirty.” — Denaun Porter, Paul Pod: Curtain Call 2, 2022
