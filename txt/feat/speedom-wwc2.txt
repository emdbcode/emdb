Trivia
The song serves as a sequel to the 2011 Tech 9ne song Worldwide Choppers.
The term “choppers” refers to a fast-paced rap style known as “chopper,” a form of Midwest hip hop used by artists such as Busta Rhymes, Twista, Tech N9ne, and Eminem.
The collaboration between Tech N9ne and Eminem was highly anticipated, as fans had wanted to hear them properly collaborate ever since they both appeared on the 1999 posse cut “The Anthem.”Tech N9ne has said he had long wanted to use “Freedom” by Richie Havens, but only decided to do so after hearing it in the Quentin Tarantino film <em>Django Unchained</em>.
Rather than payment, Eminem requested a favor in exchange for his verse, asking Tech N9ne to contribute to a Shady Records project. Tech N9ne later fulfilled this by appearing on “Beast,” alongside Busta Rhymes and KXNG Crooked, from the <em>Southpaw</em> soundtrack released later that same year.
