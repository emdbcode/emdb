Trivia
2Pac’s vocals were recorded in 1996, two weeks before his death, at Ice-T’s home studio. Eminem and the Outlawz recorded their vocals in 2003.
The song originally featured what Hen-Gee described as an “’80s rhythm-and-soul–type vibe.”
Hen-Gee revealed that 2Pac received eight beats from him and planned to record more verses for this track, along with additional songs, but their sessions were rescheduled due to Ice-T requiring Hen-Gee’s services for the upcoming Body Count album.
Hen-Gee completed the original version sometime between 1997 and 1999, enlisting West Coast rappers Spice 1 and LP to record verses, with Headstrong providing the chorus. This version was first released in 1999 on the compilation album <em>Big Star Entertainment Presents: New World Order</em>. It was later re-released in 2004 under the title “U Gotta Take It (One Day at a Time)” on Spice 1’s ninth studio album <em>The Ridah</em>.
Eminem’s version was released as a vinyl-exclusive single in March 2004, peaking at No. 80 on the Billboard Hot 100 and No. 134 on the UK Singles Chart.
