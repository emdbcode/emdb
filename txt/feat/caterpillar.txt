Words from Royce Da 5’9”
I remember I wanted [Em] to be on something because the Book of Ryan is my self-defining album. I wanted that to be the album in my catalog that I’m not supposed to be able to top, so of course I’ve gotta have my brother on that one. “Caterpillar” just spoke to me in that way, like, “You should put Marshall on this.”
Just in case he didn’t have time to do it, I also sent it to Logic. So Logic ended up doing his verse before Marshall. And I remember when I brought it to Marshall for him to listen to it, he was like, “Logic’s verse is on there already,” and I was like, “Yeah.” So when I played it for him, I just remember him going, “Okay.” And he ended up killing it.
I think he just has this thing where he wants to outdo everybody. I think that was just something that kind of put a battery in his back, lit the fire under him, because Logic killed it too. I still used that version, but it was more of a B version than the A version.
— Royce Da 5’9”, Paul Pod: Curtain Call 2, 2022
