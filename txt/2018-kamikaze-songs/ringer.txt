Trivia
Eminem kicks off the album by taking shots at Joe Budden, Lil Yachty, MGK, Iggy Azalea, Lil Pump, Lil Xan, Charlamagne tha God, Donald Trump, and “mumble rappers” in general.
In the song, Eminem mentions being visited by the Secret Service. In an interview shortly after the album’s release, he confirmed this to be true.
In 2019, the agency released a 43-page document confirming that they had, in fact, interviewed him. It revealed their interest in speaking with him was based on “threatening lyrics” from his song “Framed” from <em>Revival</em>. The Secret Service characterized Eminem as “exhibiting inappropriate behavior” and noted that he, via the rap, “threatens protectee.”
In 2023, the song was released as a 7-inch collector’s single, available exclusively on eminem.com.
[The Secret Service] came to my studio, yeah. They were just basically asking me questions about my lyrics to see if what the intent was behind them and if I was making an actual threat or just expressing myself. — Eminem, The Kamikaze Interview, 2018
