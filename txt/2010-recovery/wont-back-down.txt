Words from P!nk and Eminem
I've been a staff producer for [Dr. Dre], so I've kind of worked my way up the ranks. What got me to Eminem was working with Slaughterhouse. As soon as I worked with Slaughterhouse and did "The One" [that] really caught his attention. He loved that record. Originally, I made that for him—it just never got to him—but it ended up getting me on Recovery.
Paul [Rosenberg] called me, and I told him that "The One" was for Em. He was like, "Oh my God, how did we miss that one? Do you have any more?" I was like, "Yeah, as a matter of fact, I do." Then Dre called me, and Dre was like, "Yo, come out here, Marshall wants to meet you."
I remember we went up to the front room at Encore [Studios]. Scott Storch was there, so it was me, Eminem, Scott Storch, and Dre. I was playing beats for Eminem, and there's this beat that I played. Liz [Rodrigues] is singing the hook [that P!nk would later sing]. He picked up a pad and started writing right there on the spot, and Dre looked at me, gave me the thumbs up, and started smiling. — DJ Khalil, Get It Done Entertainment, 2013
It was one of them tracks where I felt like I just heard [P!nk] on it. And I've always respected what she does. She’s kind of a rebel to me, you know, and obviously she's talented, so she did her thing. — Eminem, BBC Radio 1, 2010
Originally we had another vocalist, Liz Rodrigues, who is in my group, but she was already on “25 To Life” and “Almost Famous.” So we were thinking, “Who else could we get for this record that would actually fit?” When [Em] brought up P!nk, I thought she would kill it. He set up the session in Malibu, we did the session, and she was done in like 20 minutes. She nailed it in her own way. I was kind of married to the original, but once I heard what she did and how she put her stamp on it, it was perfect, because she just brought the attitude out.  — DJ Khalil, MTV, 2010
Trivia
“Won’t Back Down” marks the first Eminem song to feature a pop star; the second was “Love The Way You Lie,” which appears later on the same album.
The song was later featured in the video game Call Of Duty: Black Ops and in the first trailer for Mission: Impossible – Ghost Protocol.
