Words from Jim Jonsin
Me and Steve McEwan actually wrote the song, we actually did it here in New York on guitar — it was just a guitar vocal on an iPhone. We took the idea later in Miami, like three months after, and produced the track. [My manager] had me strip down the verses, take them out, and make the track a little more hip-hop, and he sent it out. The chorus was based off of a chase, a guy who's chasing after a woman that he's crazy about. He loves her; she's everything to him. I'm a rocket ship aiming at her heart, her heart's the moon — I'm going full-on. I didn't really believe that it would end up on Eminem’s album, let alone be a single. — Jim Jonsin, MTV, 2011

Trivia

The song was promoted as a streaming single by Interscope Records via SoundCloud on June 7, 2011, between the two Bad Meets Evil singles “Fast Lane” and “Lighters,” released in May and July respectively. The track was also serviced to radio, but did not receive an official physical or downloadable single release.

The song’s video was filmed in Santa Clarita, California and stars former adult film actress Sasha Grey as Eminem’s love interest. The video was directed by Joseph Kahn, who also directed the videos for “Without Me,” “We Made You,” and “Love the Way You Lie.”
