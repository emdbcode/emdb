Words from Eminem
That song is about my addiction, and my mind frame at the time. I don't feel like a recluse now. I do go out and do things – it's just hard. You've got to take an entourage. It's a pain in the ass. When I didn't have a record out for four or five years, I was taking little trips down to see my great-aunt Edna, before she passed. I knew it was getting close – she was in her 90s – and I wanted to spend as much time with her as I could. Not having a record out, I could stop at a gas station, go places and not get recognized. That was actually a pretty good feeling.  — Eminem, Rolling Stone, 2010

Trivia
While Recovery is known for dropping many of Eminem’s typical tropes, “Going Through Changes” uses a vocal sample in the chorus—something Eminem has included once on every album since The Marshall Mathers LP (except Encore, which had two)—showing that this particular trope remains, though it wasn’t released as a single like the others (apart from that extra one on Encore).
