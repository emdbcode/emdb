Words from
I think that might have been one of the beats that Havoc sent for Recovery—somehow I got a CD of a couple more of his beats. I brought that one in one day, and we just started fucking with it. I think I laid the first verse. I think Royce was up in the studio or something and we started doing the record and decided to trade-off … After it was done, I thought about it working as the intro. It might have been the next day or something, coming back we decided what we were going to do with it and I went and recorded the part about, “Welcome to the album.” — Eminem, Complex, 2011
Being involved with Eminem’s Recovery made it possible for me to be involved with the Bad Meets Evil. I’m always constantly hitting Em with tracks. I would say I sent him that beat a while ago. Those were tracks that I sent trying to get on Relapse.<br>I made the beat in NYC in my crib. I’m always digging for samples, weird-sounding things that people never used before. Things that might make you go, “What the fuck is that? That shit is wack,” or, “That shit is dope.” I just fuck with all ends of the spectrum when it comes to samples. I was fucking with some Gregorian chants because no one ever fucked with it. It sounds real eerie, hard, and dark, and it just stuck. — Havoc, Complex, 2011
