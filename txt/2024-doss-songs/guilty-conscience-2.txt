Trivia
“Guilty Conscience 2” serves as a standalone sequel to “Guilty Conscience” from the <em>Slim Shady LP</em>. The difference is that it features a back-and-forth between the evil conscience (again, Slim Shady) and the host—this time only one person, Eminem himself, as opposed to a good and evil conscience debating the decisions of three separate hosts.
The song is preceded by the “Breaking News” skit, the last of several conceptual setups for this track. Eminem posted the following warning on social media: “Public service announcement: <em>The Death of Slim Shady</em> is a conceptual album; therefore, if you listen to songs out of order, they might not make sense. Enjoy.”
Although it’s not the album’s final track, the song serves as the climax of the album’s “death of Slim Shady/coup de grâce” concept.
The song’s beat is very similar to the “Trouble” beat, heard earlier on the album. Fredwreck and Dem Jointz served as producers on both tracks, with Eminem contributing as a producer on this redo.
Words from Fredwreck
[Em] is still making great records. The Death of Slim Shady is probably one of my favorite records from him. […] People were hitting me up like, “Is that A.I.?” I’m like, “No, that’s him doing it” — he’s doing the old voice and the [present-day] voice. That’s not no studio tricks.
— Fredwreck, Habibi House Podcast, 2025
