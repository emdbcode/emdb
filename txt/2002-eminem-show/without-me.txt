Trivia
The basic instrumental for “Without Me” was created in roughly half an hour during the early hours of a summer night in 2001. Around 2:30 a.m., Eminem played a simple drum track recorded by DJ Head while Jeff Bass experimented with keyboard sounds, eventually landing on a bass effect that fit. Bass heard two notes he liked and began repeating them over the beat. After listening for a few bars, Eminem improvised the melody that would, humming it to Bass. Bass then picked up a saxophone and played the melody, and by 3 a.m. the music for the song had been written.
Eminem later interpolated the “Guess who’s back” segment from “Without Me” for his 2024 single “Houdini.” Its music video also borrows numerous elements from the “Without Me” video.
In June 2024, “Without Me” experienced a 309% increase in sales following the release of “Houdini." By October that year, the song surpassed "Lose Yourself" to become Eminem's most-streamed song on Spotify, with over 2.9 billion streams as of 2026.
The song currently holds the record for Eminem's longest-charting song on the Billboard Global Excl. U.S. chart, spending over 200 weeks on the ranking.
In the summer of 2002, a dance instrumental remix titled “E,” incorporating the song’s saxophone riff, was released by Drunkenmunky and charted in the top ten in several European countries.
