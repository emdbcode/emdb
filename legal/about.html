About EMDb

EMDb (Eminem Music Database) is a solo, fan-made project built to organise and explore the full discography of Eminem in one structured place.

This is not a team project. There is no organisation behind it. Every page has been built by a single fan. Countless hours have gone into researching, organising, designing, and coding every part of the site.

Why EMDb Exists

EMDb was built out of frustration with how scattered and inconsistent music information can be online. User-generated platforms are incredibly useful, but with a mix of interpretations and a reliance on users to add content, information can become inaccurate, disorganized, or remain undocumented, and Eminem's discography is no exception.

EMDb was created to bring everything together in a single, structured, compact format. The goal is to build the most accurate online resource dedicated to Eminem's discography. With thousands of interconnected pages covering officially released songs, albums, singles, samples, production credits, and much more, EMDb is designed as a lasting resource for both current and future generations of Eminem fans.

User Contribution

EMDb also includes user ratings and reviews to help reflect how the Eminem community views each release.

The aim is to build a clearer picture of how the discography is experienced by fans as a whole.

User XP

EMDb rewards participation through an XP (Experience Points) system. As you explore the site and contribute to the community, you will earn XP and level up your profile.

There are 11 levels to achieve, progressing from Level 1 through Level 10, before reaching the highest rank: Stan.

How To Earn XP

You can earn XP by contributing across the site:

  • Rate an album - 10 XP
  • Rate a song - 1 XP
  • Review an album - 10 XP
  • Post a thought on a song - 1 XP
  • Choose your Top 5 albums - 10 XP total (2 XP per selection)

More ways to earn XP may be added as EMDb continues to grow.

Unofficial Project

EMDb is an unofficial fan-made website.

All artist names, album titles, song titles, album artwork, and other references are used for identification, informational, and commentary purposes.

What’s Next?

EMDb is an ongoing project and will continue to grow over time. New releases, features, and content will continue to be added with the aim of making EMDb the most complete and reliable resource for exploring Eminem's catalogue.

FAQ

Why aren't leaked songs included?

Leaked songs are not included because they are not officially released material and are therefore not considered part of Eminem's official discography. A leaks section is planned for the future, but it will not host the songs themselves - it will only provide information and documentation relating to them.

Can I contribute or suggest corrections?

Yes. If you notice an error or have information that could improve EMDb, please visit the contact page to get in touch.

How can I add an album review?

Visit the album you would like to review, click the album title, and select "Add a Review." If you wish to edit or delete a submitted review, simply repeat the same process.
